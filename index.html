Livingstone is Livingstone is a small coastal town situated in the Izabal department of Guatemala. It's known for its rich Garifuna culture, which is a mix of West and Central African, Caribbean, and Arawak Indigenous cultures. The town is surrounded by lush tropical vegetation and has stunning beaches with crystal-clear waters. Livingstone is a popular tourist destination and offers a range of activities, such as snorkeling, scuba diving, fishing, and hiking. The town's unique cultural heritage and natural beauty make it a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic and unforgettable experience in Guatemala.

The Local Communities

Livingston, Guatemala is a unique town with a vibrant Afro-Caribbean culture. The local community is primarily made up of Garifuna people, who are descendants of African, Caribbean, and indigenous groups. The Garifuna culture is known for its music, dance, and cuisine, which blend African, Caribbean, and indigenous influences. The people of Livingston are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and many make a living from fishing, tourism, and handicrafts. The town has a laid-back atmosphere and is a great place to relax and learn about the local culture.

Activities

The town offers a range of outdoor activities, including swimming, snorkeling, and kayaking, as well as hiking and visiting nearby waterfalls. Visitors can also take boat tours to explore the nearby mangrove forests and see local wildlife, such as manatees and dolphins. Livingston is also a great place to sample the local cuisine, which features a mix of Caribbean and Guatemalan flavors, and to experience the vibrant Garifuna culture through music and dance performances.
